About Me and My Service

Howdy! Welcome to my personal website; my name is Arya Rahmanian and I am a Computer Engineering student at Texas A&M University. Throughout my experience as a student, I’ve become interested in pursuing Computer Architecture. I’m inspired by how this field combines software and hardware into how a computer will work, almost like building a puzzle but with an exponentially higher learning curve. Starting when the first computer was built in WWII, this field is always developing and becoming more complex as there is always a need for more computing power in today’s world. There is never a dull moment with computer architecture.

A small project I once worked on for a class is that I built a CPU entirely in Verilog. Granted, this was a CPU from the 1980s, but it could still perform the basic functions that a modern day CPU could perform, likely much slower.

Even though my courses take up most of my time, I'm still able to give back to my community that has helped me so much. Over the years I've been involved in Eta Kappa Ku, Arab Aggie Engineering Association, UNICEF, Photography Club, PALS, and was a counselor for a local religious camp in Dallas called CFT. My most fond memory of service would be my experience as a staff at the camp CFT, Change For Tomorrow. The members of the camp were 12-14 years old and I was responsible for creating programs throughout the day as well as making sure everyone was getting along. I enjoyed it so much because I was given a lot of responsibility over the members, and it was able to teach me a lot about being a leader and having others follow what you say. Not only that, I also had a great time working with these junior youth and I’m proud of the work I accomplished.
